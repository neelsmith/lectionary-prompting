CTS URNs have 5 components separated by colons. We want to use the 4th and 5th components to find a passage in the Latin Vulgate text of the Bible. If we have a CTS URN "urn:cts:compnov:bible.genesis.vulgate:1.1" we want to look at the fourth component "bible.genesis.vulgate" and the fifth component "1.1".

The fourth component has three parts separated by periods. Its second component identifies the name of a Biblical book in lower case letters. In our example, that is "genesis". The third part identifies a version of the text. In our example, that is "vulgate". 

THe fifth component of the URN is the passage reference, here "1.1".
